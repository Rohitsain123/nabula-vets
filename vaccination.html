Vaccinations

Vaccinations are key in keeping your pet healthy and preventing them catching life-threatening disease.

Our veterinary team is dedicated to providing comprehensive vaccination services tailored to your pet's individual needs and lifestyle. This includes not only dogs and cats but also rabbits and ferrets.

BOOK NOW
Why should I vaccinate my pet?

Vaccines are affective as the expose your pet’s immune system to an inactive strain of infectious agents. This assists their body to create immune cells that are specifically designed to respond effectively when exposed to the disease. Vaccines are also relatively painless, quick and cost-effective to administer.

What are core and non-core vaccines?

Core vaccines are often vaccines considered essential for your pet’s health, whilst non-core vaccines are optional, and may be recommended for your pet depending on their lifestyle.

Our vets will discuss the suitable vaccines for your pet during the consultation.

Core Dog Vaccines
FVRCP: A combination vaccine that protects against Feline Viral Rhinotracheitis, Calicivirus, and Panleukopenia. Rabies: Legally required in most states, for both indoor and outdoor cats Feline Leukemia Virus: A virus transmitted through cat bites during fights. It’s recommended for kittens even if they will not be going outdoors.
Non-Core Dog Vaccines
Bordetella: A disease more commonly known as kennel cough and is required by dog boarding and daycare facilities. Lyme: Lyme Disease is carried by ticks and can be useful for dogs that visit areas, such as woodland areas, with high tick exposure. Canine Influenza Virus: Also called dog flu, a contagious virus that causes respiratory issues.
Cat Core Vaccines
FVRCP: A combination vaccine that protects against Feline Viral Rhinotracheitis, Calicivirus, and Panleukopenia. Rabies: Legally required in most states, for both indoor and outdoor cats Feline Leukemia Virus: A virus transmitted through cat bites during fights. It’s recommended for kittens even if they will not be going outdoors.

When to vaccinate?

Puppies Schedule

8 weeks:

Distemper vaccine, Leptospirosis, Bordetella vaccine, Lyme vaccine & Canine Influenza Virus vaccine

12 weeks:

Distemper vaccine, Leptospirosis, Bordetella vaccine, Lyme vaccine & Canine Influenza Virus vaccine

Booster:

Kitten Schedule

8 weeks:

FVRCP vaccine and Leukemia vaccines

12 weeks:

FVRCP vaccine, Leukemia vaccine& Rabies vaccine

Adult Dog Schedule

Rabies: One year after the initial puppy vaccine, then every three years

Distemper: Every three years

Bordetella: Every year

Leptospirosis: Every year

Booster:

Canine influenza: Every year

Lyme disease: Every year

Adult Cat Schedule

FVRCP: Every year

Rabies: Every three years

Feline Leukemia Virus: Every year
